The Future History of the British Isles

​by Hugh Dichmont

Every generation thinks it is living through the apocalypse. One day, somebody will be right.

Britain in the near future: Nottinghamshire has voted to leave England. London has declared war on Yorkshire. And Sarah... Sarah is breaking up with Graham, because Graham is a ****.

‘Future History’ is an irreverent sketch-show-storytelling-hybrid that takes a sideways glance at global Armageddon, fake news and mass-ignorance-hysteria; mythologising a future in which the very worst has happened. The end is nigh, so dig a hole.

The War On Terry

​by Hugh Dichmont

Photography by Mark Dawson

My girlfriend thinks I’m obsessed with death. Well maybe I am, but only as much as the next rotting sack of bones.

It’s Terry’s birthday. At 27, he is now older than his older brother Danny.

With a shoebox full of dead ladybirds, Terry reminisces over their childhood, whilst fighting to come to terms with his regrets over how things could have been different. A darkly comic monologue about boys, Christmas socks and not growing up.

Previewed at Nottingham Playhouse
Performed at Buxton Fringe, Landor_Space (London) and the Television Workshop (Nottingham)
